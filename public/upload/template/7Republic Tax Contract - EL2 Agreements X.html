THE REPUBLIC TAX RELIEF METHOD
RTR utilizes a team of attorneys, Certified Public Accountants (CPAs), IRS Enrolled Agents (EAs), and other
professionals to perform tax resolution, mediation, and preparation services. At the commencement of this
agreement, RTR will file a Power of Attorney (POA) on behalf of the CLIENT with the appropriate taxing
authority(s).
Once the POA has been executed, RTR will conduct a thorough investigation—using financial information provided
by the client—to determine CLIENT'S outstanding tax liability and whether CLIENT is in filing compliance. Based
on the findings of the investigation, RTR will prepare a final resolution tax plan in accordance with the legal
collections options provided by the Internal Revenue Service. This plan will be prepared, submitted, and negotiated
with the appropriate taxing authority(s).
RTR will provide the best tax resolution possible to CLIENT, based on CLIENT'S individual circumstance.
Resolution may include an installment agreement, Offer in Compromise, penalty abatement, Currently Non-
Collectible status, etc.; however, not every type of resolution is available to every client, and RTR will solely
determine the best resolution based on the CLIENT'S individual circumstance.
Each taxing authority decides the outcome of all requests made before it, and no specific resolution is ever
guaranteed. In addition, none of the services can be relied upon to detect errors or illegal acts that may exist;
however, RTR will inform CLIENT of any such matters as they occur. The amount of an Offer in Compromise is not
based upon a national or overall average of tax debt forgiveness rates, but on the unique facts of each taxpayer. RTR
will perform its duties in a manner consistent with the degree of care and skill ordinarily exercised by members of
the same profession currently practicing under similar circumstances, in providing services under this Agreement.
