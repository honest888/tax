  • File Power of Attorney (IRS Form 2848)
  • Request a "stay of enforcement" with an IRS Collections division representative in an attempt to defend against Enforced Collection Activity, if needed.
  • Order all records of account, transcripts and income information on file with the IRS; immediately after, we will contact the IRS's Practitioners' Priority Service and receive an oral explanation of the IRS coding and classifications for your overall taxpayer profile.

Phase 2: Final Resolution Proposal

Summary

Per our initial conversation, you owe approximately {{DEBT}} in back taxes. We discussed several options for final resolution of your tax liabilities, and below is a description of the programs that may be available once we have obtained your results of our investigation and analysis. No fees will be incurred for implementation of a resolution strategy as described below until after your IRS Investigation and Financial Analysis are completed and you have received a Final Investigation Consultation.

POSSIBLE RESOLUTION OPTIONS

Individual results will vary depending on the results of our investigation. We make no guarantees that these options will apply to your situation and these options listed are meant strictly for explanation purposes only.

Offer in Compromise (OIC)

This program allows taxpayers to settle their entire tax debt for a lower amount based entirely on what they can afford to pay. It requires full disclosure of all household income and expenses and an analysis of net equity in assets.

1

An OIC for an individual taxpayer typically takes one month to prepare and six months for the IRS to process and accept. An OIC for a self-employed or corporate taxpayer typically takes about two months to prepare and anywhere from nine months to over a year for the IRS to process. Although these time frames are subject to change based on the time of year and IRS staffing. Very few of these are accepted by the IRS relative to those who apply. This is a rarely accepted option and requires special circumstances.

Average Time Frame: 9-12 months

Installment Agreement (IA)

This program works in two ways. If it is a Hardship Payment Installment Agreement, taxpayers can pay less than what they owe over time and the remaining debt will expire (unlike the OIC program, where taxpayers typically pay what they can afford to pay all at once). The monthly payment is calculated based upon what they can afford to pay each month.

A Full Payment Installment Agreement is an agreement between the taxpayer and the IRS to pay the debt in full, over the length of time the IRS can legally collect the debt (typically 10 years) or less, depending on the agreement. In order to reduce your debt with any form of Installment Agreement program, we can submit a Penalty Abatement Petition with the IRS Appeals Division once we have established compliance.

Average Time Frame: 1-6 months

Currently Non Collectible (CNC)

Currently Non-Collectible (CNC) status is a determination by IRS Collections personnel to suspend collections due to economic hardship. The IRS agrees to suspend collections (i.e., refrain from issuing a wage or bank levy) so long as the taxpayer's income remains at the same level as when CNC status was approved and the taxpayer files and pays taxes on time each year.

Average Time Frame: 3-6 months
